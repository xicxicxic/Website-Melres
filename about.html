History

Starting as Mellares, Latin for "Land of honey", Melres was a hub for beekeping and agriculture in its origin due to the fertility of the land.
Being occupied by the romans, the land started getting recognition with the creation of a agricultural community, being one of the biggest in the margin of the Douro river.
The geographical limits of the town were discussed as early as 1258, with the then king of Portugal D. Afonso III using the old community as a reference for the town. Only in 1369, D. Fernando I changed the limits of the town to those still present today.
In 1834, the town was downgraded from Concelho to Freguesia.
